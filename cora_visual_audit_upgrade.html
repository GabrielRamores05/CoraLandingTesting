Visual problems I can see in your screenshot
Critical — first 3 seconds
The giant orange "Cora" logo dominates your hero
In your screenshot, the very first thing a visitor sees is your logo (huge, centered) — not a value proposition. Your brain processes images before text. Right now, visitors spend their first 2 seconds reading a brand name they don't know yet. That attention should go to your headline.
Now
Giant logo → eyebrow text → headline → description → buttons
Should be
Small nav logo + Outcome headline (huge) → subheadline → CTA button → trust signals
Critical — credibility
Stats show "0+" and "0+" — looks broken
I can clearly see in your screenshot: the hero stats area shows "0+" for at least two of the three metrics. Even if this is a JS animation that starts at 0, on a slow connection or before JS loads, visitors literally see "0+ cooperatives served" and "0+ members." That destroys credibility instantly.
✦ Fix: Hardcode the real numbers in HTML. Let JS animate them up, but the fallback must always be the real stat.
High — visual hierarchy
Two equal-weight hero buttons — no clear primary action
Both buttons look similar in visual weight. "See How It Works" (outline) and "Get Offline Cora" (filled dark) compete for attention. When everything is equally important, nothing is important. Your #1 goal should dominate visually.
Now
[SEE HOW IT WORKS] [GET OFFLINE CORA] — equal weight
Should be
[BOOK FREE DEMO →] big orange filled · "or view offline edition" small text link below
High — visual noise
The "Challenges" section cards look disconnected and small
From the screenshot, the 4 challenge cards are small white squares with icons that are hard to read at a glance. The active card (dark green) is only subtly different. The grid feels like a feature list, not an emotional connection to the reader's pain.
✦ Fix: Make each card bigger. Use a large pain-point headline inside each card instead of tiny text. The active state should be dramatically different — maybe an illustrated icon or a full-bleed dark card.
High — wasted real estate
"Our Purpose" section is a huge amount of white space for very little content
Your Purpose section has about 60% white space. One label, a giant "Our Purpose" title, a thin line, one sentence, and one attribution. That's a full viewport of space for 2 sentences. This kills scroll momentum and makes the page feel unfinished.
✦ Fix: Merge Purpose into your Hero or About section. Or expand it with 3 value pillars: Accuracy · Compliance · Transparency — each with an icon and 2-line description.
High — confusing layout
"Your Cooperative Your Choice" section has tiny laptop images
The Offline vs Online comparison shows two laptop images that are too small to convey the product's quality. Visitors can't actually see what the software looks like. The "OFFLINE" and "ONLINE" tab labels are so small they're barely readable.
✦ Fix: Full-width comparison. Left: large laptop mockup of offline UI. Right: large browser mockup of online UI. Big feature bullets under each. Clear "Get Offline" and "Try Online" CTAs.
Medium — mobile
The topbar contact info is gone on mobile but leaves a dark green stripe
Your topbar with the phone number and address is CSS-hidden on mobile but the dark green bar still shows (just empty). This wastes 40px of valuable above-the-fold space on mobile where every pixel matters.
✦ Fix: Set the entire topbar to display:none on mobile, not just the contact items. Or keep it but show only "0962 807 3120" (the most important info).
Medium — trust
Partner logos are too faded (28% opacity grayscale)
Your carousel logos use filter: grayscale(100%) opacity: 0.28 — they're almost invisible. Social proof only works if people can actually see and recognize the logos. Faded logos signal "we're embarrassed by these partners" rather than "look who trusts us."
✦ Fix: opacity: 0.55 minimum. On hover: opacity 1 + color (you already have this). Or keep them in color at 70% opacity to make them more recognizable.

Missing — High Impact
No pricing section (even a range or "contact us")
Cooperative managers are budget-conscious. The absence of pricing creates anxiety — their mind defaults to "probably expensive." Even "Contact us for pricing tailored to your cooperative size" with a CTA removes this blocker.

Missing — High Impact
No FAQ section
Your buyers (board chairs, treasurers, accountants) have specific objections: "Is this CDA-compliant?" "What if we have no internet?" "Do we need IT staff?" "Can we migrate our existing data?" None of these are answered on the page.
✦ Add 5–6 FAQ items above the footer. Answer: CDA compliance, offline capability, data migration, technical support, pricing, and data security.

Visitors don't understand the journey from "interested" to "using CORA." A simple 3-step flow — "Book Demo → Get Setup → Go Live" — reduces perceived effort and makes committing feel less scary.
